01 / The Challenge

A differentiated product.
A website that lost every lead in the first scroll.

When Fiter came to Vellumwire, the site was functionally live but strategically broken. The product was validated by USP and Mackenzie, trusted by the Brazilian Navy, and had measured over 500,000 people. None of that was landing.

P 01
The value proposition wasn't landing.
A visitor arriving from Google couldn't understand what Fiter did within the first 10 seconds, let alone why it was different from GPTW or any other HR tool. The site described the product. It didn't make the case for it.
P 02
Two businesses. One confused site.
Fiter serves two structurally different markets: corporate HR and higher education. Different buyers, different problems, different regulatory pressures, different buying triggers. The site treated both as one audience and converted neither.
P 03
Five products. Zero indexed presence.
Pulse, Pesquisa de Clima, Agente NR1, Ouvidoria, Guia Prático, plus a dedicated Education product built around the IFE. None had their own page, their own keyword cluster, or a clear reason to choose them over alternatives.
P 04
Invisible on Google.
Fiter had zero presence for any commercial keyword in their category: HR tech, employee engagement, pulse survey, turnover prediction, NR1 compliance, student dropout prevention. A deeptech validated by USP and the Brazilian Navy was generating no organic traffic.

03 / The Discovery

Two findings.
They shaped everything.

The discovery session with founder Sergio Amad surfaced two strategic insights that drove every architecture and copy decision that followed.

Finding 01 — The 8-click argument

8 clicks. 2 minutes. 87% response rate.

Employees complete their monthly check-in in 8 clicks, 2 minutes, via WhatsApp — no app, no login. That number isn't a UX convenience. It's a business argument: high adoption drives high-quality data, which drives AI predictions you can actually trust, which means a business owner retains people before the resignation letter arrives. The entire value chain collapses into one number. We made it the anchor of the site.

Finding 02 — Two buyers, one confused site

Corporate HR and Education are structurally different markets.

Corporate HR buyers worry about turnover costs (R$48k per dismissed employee), NR1 compliance deadlines, and invisible attrition. Education buyers worry about dropout rates (47% of Brazilian students don't finish), CNE 3/2025 compliance, and first-year retention. A single site selling to both was converting neither. The architecture decision was clear: two tracks, buyers self-select on the first screen.

04 / The Work

Four phases. One site that finally does the selling.

The rebuild started with positioning and ended with a fully indexed, dual-track website. Every decision was tied to one outcome: the right buyer lands, understands the value in seconds, and books a demo.

01
Discovery & positioning

Built the argument before writing a word.

A structured audit and stakeholder session to identify real buying triggers, real objections, and the differentiators that weren't being communicated. The 8-click insight and the two-market finding came out of this phase and drove all subsequent decisions.

02
Information architecture

Two tracks. Buyers self-select on the first screen.

The homepage hero gained two CTAs — "Sou uma Empresa" and "Sou uma Universidade" — routing each buyer into a dedicated track. Each of the five products received its own indexed page, its own keyword cluster, and its own conversion argument.

03
Copywriting

Written to rank first. Then to convert.

Seven pages: every H1, H2, body block, comparison table, FAQ, meta title, and meta description. The homepage headline leads with the invisible problem, not the product name. The RH page includes a direct comparison table against Pesquisa Tradicional and GPTW across 7 dimensions. The science credentials were repositioned from a footer footnote to a mid-funnel buying argument.

04
UX direction & visual system

Dashboard mockups that communicate product depth without a demo.

Interface mockups for Pulse, Agente NR1, Pesquisa de Clima, Ouvidoria, and the Education IFE dashboard — showing real product states: live scores, AI-generated action plans, turnover alerts, CNE compliance status. Structured enough for enterprise buyers. Modern enough to signal a tech platform, not an HR consultancy.
