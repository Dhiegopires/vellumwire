01 / The Problem

A beautiful website.
Zero leads from it.

They'd invested heavily in a site that read like it was written by a committee who'd never met a client. Polished. Expensively useless.

01
The homepage was a vanity mirror.
Eight paragraphs about "legacy of excellence." Zero mention of what a client gets. Hero image: a stock gavel.
02
The CTA was buried on page four.
No phone above the fold. An 11-field contact form, including fax number.
03
160+ case wins appeared nowhere on the homepage.
Their "Awards" page had three items, listed in Times New Roman.
04
63% of traffic was mobile. The site had horizontal scroll.
A hamburger menu with 14 items. Key content cut off on every screen size below desktop.

"We don't need a new website we need more referrals." We asked what a prospect sees when they Google the firm after a referral. He went quiet.

03 / The Work

Four weeks. Three bets.

We didn't redesign the whole site. We identified the three highest-impact changes and executed them surgically.

01
Week 1: Kill the brochure

Rewrote every word above the fold.

Replaced 340 words of institutional copy with a 12-word headline and a proof bar. Credibility in 3 seconds not 3 pages.

02
Week 2–3: Rebuild the intake

Replaced the 11-field form with a 2-step qualifier.

Step 1: select issue type. Step 2: name + email + one sentence. Click-to-call on every page. Tripled after-hours enquiries.

03
Week 3–4: Prove everything

Social proof that doesn't look like marketing.

Case results as data. Named testimonials from CFOs. Media mentions inline, not on a page nobody visits. Every proof point near a CTA.
