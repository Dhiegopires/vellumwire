01 / The Situation

The product had credibility.
The website gave it away.

Selo H certifies companies on workplace mental health — a high-trust, compliance-driven market where legal credibility and scientific rigor are table stakes. The certification is grounded in NR-01, Lei 14.831/24, WHO guidelines, and ICD-11. The old website contradicted all of that.

P 01
No clear primary action.
The path from "landing" to "requesting certification" wasn't obvious. Multiple competing CTAs diluted intent — visitors had nowhere decisive to go.
P 02
Weak information hierarchy.
The most persuasive content — legal compliance, certified companies, the 4-step process — was buried or poorly sequenced. The site answered the wrong questions first.
P 03
Copy that described instead of persuaded.
The original copy explained the certification. It didn't make the case for it. No urgency, no cost of inaction, no proof that any of this was real.
P 04
Performance that undermined trust.
A compliance-focused certification product cannot afford a slow, visually unstable website. First impressions are already a judgment call — the old site was losing that call.
P 05
Visual language out of sync with the brand.
Certification implies rigor. Inconsistent spacing, generic typography, and no clear brand personality made the product feel smaller than it was.
P 06
Social proof that was impossible to feel.
Certified companies like Alelo, Amil, and Cogna are genuine credibility signals. They were present but not featured — easy to miss, impossible to act on.

03 / The Work

Strategy before pixels. Five bets.

The rebuild started with one question: what does a compliance-minded HR director need to believe before they request a certification? That answer shaped everything — narrative arc, section order, proof architecture, and copy tone.

01
Lead with the outcome

The hero opens with what the reader already wants.

Not features — proof that their company takes mental health seriously. The certification is the mechanism. The outcome is the lead.

02
Compliance proof above the fold

NR-01, Lei 14.831/24, WHO — front and center.

Legal codes aren't footnotes — they're the primary buying trigger for an HR or legal decision-maker. They appear immediately beneath the headline, not in the footer.

03
Rigor as an asset

A 4-step process that earns trust instead of explaining it.

The certification process is genuinely rigorous — which is an asset, not a barrier. Each step is made to feel purposeful and achievable, not bureaucratic and opaque.

04
Social proof as the closing argument

Alelo, Amil, Arcor, Claro, Cogna — not a footer drop.

These names appear right before the contact form. They're the strongest proof that the certification is real, selective, and worth pursuing.

05
One CTA, unambiguous

"Solicitar certificação" — the only ask, repeated throughout.

No newsletter, no download, no alternative path. Every section points toward one decision. The page ends when the visitor decides.

Key Insight

A certification platform must be as credible as the certification itself.

Every compliance-market product is selling trust before it sells features. If the website doesn't feel airtight, no amount of legal references will move a risk-averse buyer. Presentation is proof.
